Faith with Understanding

Believing blindly is not what Jesus called us to do—He told us to have faith like children, not ignorance like fools. Childlike faith is pure and trusting, but mature faith is also rooted in truth and understanding. In a world where every belief is questioned and every truth is challenged, we cannot afford to say, "I just believe." We must know why we believe.

Many people reject Christianity not because they've truly understood it, but because of misunderstandings and half-truths.

Common objections include:

  • If God is so good, why is there so much suffering?
  • The Bible was written by men, how can it be trusted?
  • All religions lead to the same God, don't they?
  • Science disproves the God of the Bible

Understanding these challenges—and the truth behind them—is part of loving God with all your mind (Matthew 22:37). Faith isn't fragile; it's built to be tested.

"Jesus said unto him, Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy mind." -Matthew 22:37

We as followers of Christ should always be able to give an answer to why we believe and should always be able to give the gospel to someone at a moments noticed

"'preach the word; be instant in season, out of season; reprove, rebuke, exhort with all longsuffering and doctrine." -2 Timothy 4:2

Defending common objections

If God is Good, why is there so much suffering

Many of athiests have asked this question as a "gotcha question". I will not lie, this is one of the hardest questions to answer on a personal level. Theologically we can give very good answers to why this is but an athiest who does not believe the bible to begin with will not appreciate these answers. This questions adresses two different aspects of creation we need to look at:

  • Why does God permit moral evils toward one another?
  • Why does God permit natural evils to happen to innocent people?

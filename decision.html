Still Deciding?

This is the most important decision of your life. Postponing it is itself a decision.

You’ve heard the gospel — you know what Christ has done. But maybe you’re hesitating. Maybe you’re not sure you’re ready, or you feel like you need to “get your life together first.”

The truth is, no one is ever ready in that sense. Salvation isn’t about you fixing yourself — it’s about admitting that you can’t, and trusting the One who can.

“Behold, now is the accepted time; behold, now is the day of salvation.” — 2 Corinthians 6:2

Every heartbeat is a gift of mercy. None of us are promised another day. To delay is to choose uncertainty when God offers assurance.

Why This Decision Can’t Wait

If a house is on fire, you don’t wait to walk out when it’s more convenient — you run. The gospel isn’t something to consider when life gets quiet or comfortable. It’s a message for now.

  • Life is uncertain. None of us know how long we have.
  • Sin is deadly. It separates us from the God who made us.
  • Christ’s offer is open. But it won’t last forever.

God’s grace is calling. The door is open — but it must be entered.

Common Questions

We all wrestle with doubts and questions. Tap to expand — you can fill in the answers later.

1) How do I know if I’m truly ready to be saved?

2) What if I’ve done too much wrong for God to forgive me?

3) Do I have to change before coming to Jesus?

4) How do I know this is real and not just emotion?

5) What if I fail again after becoming a Christian?

6) What if I don’t feel anything when I pray?

Counting the Cost

Jesus never hid the cost of following Him. He didn’t invite people to an easy life — He called them to die to self and live for Him.

“If any man will come after me, let him deny himself, and take up his cross daily, and follow me.” — Luke 9:23

Following Christ means:

  • Turning away from sin — not perfectly, but sincerely.
  • Placing Him above everything — even comfort and approval.
  • Trusting that He is worth infinitely more than what you leave behind.

The Christian life is not about adding religion; it’s about surrender — a new heart, a new master, a new purpose. It costs you your pride, but gives you peace. It costs you your sin, but gives you salvation.

The Cross Before You

“Whosoever shall call upon the name of the Lord shall be saved.” — Romans 10:13

You don’t need fancy words — only a sincere heart. God already knows what you need. If you’re still undecided, take time to read the Gospel again, pray honestly, and ask God to show you the truth.

But remember — neutrality is not an option. To remain undecided is to say “not yet” to the One who died for you.

“Choose you this day whom ye will serve.” — Joshua 24:15

Next Steps

  • Talk to a believer you trust.
  • Open your Bible — start with the book of John.
  • Ask God to give you a new heart and the courage to follow Christ.

Don’t wait for a better time. Today is mercy — tomorrow is not promised.

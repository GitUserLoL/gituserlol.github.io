Assurance of Salvation

Confidence rooted in Christ’s promise, not our performance.

The Assurance of Eternal Life

Are you 100% sure that you have eternal life?

If you are struggling with this question, it is essential to look at the Scriptures to see where the certainty of salvation rests. The Bible teaches that your security is not based on your ability to hold onto God, but on God's power to hold onto you and the definitive nature of the gift He has given.

The Nature of Eternal Life

The first point to consider is the word eternal

  • When the Lord Jesus Christ gives life, it is defined by its unending nature. He stated, "And I give unto them eternal life; and they shall never perish, neither shall any man pluck them out of my hand." (John 10:28).
  • If this life could be lost, then it was never truly "eternal" to begin with; it would be conditional life. The Bible emphasizes that eternal life is a gift received by grace through faith in Christ, not something earned by works (Ephesians 2:8-9). A gift cannot be taken back if the giver is faithful and the gift is inherently permanent.
  • The Apostle John wrote, "And this is the record, that God hath given to us eternal life, and this life is in his Son. He that hath the Son hath life; and he that hath not the Son of God hath not life." (1 John 5:11-12). The possession of the Son means the possession of the life He gives.

Security by Divine Action

Your security is established and maintained by the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit, making it a triune guarantee.

1. Secured by the Son's Hand

Jesus Christ guarantees that the Father's will is for Him to lose "nothing" of those given to Him (John 6:39). The believer is held in two hands simultaneously:

  • "And I give unto them eternal life; and they shall never perish, neither shall any man pluck them out of my hand." (John 10:28)
  • "My Father, which gave them me, is greater than all; and no man is able to pluck them out of my Father's hand." (John 10:29)

2. Sealed by the Holy Spirit

When a person trusts in Christ, the Holy Spirit immediately indwells and marks that person as belonging to God. This action is a seal that lasts until the final redemption.

  • "In whom ye also trusted, after that ye heard the word of truth, the gospel of your salvation: in whom also after that ye believed, ye were sealed with that holy Spirit of promise, Which is the earnest of our inheritance, until the redemption of the purchased possession, unto the praise of his glory." (Ephesians 1:13-14)
  • The Holy Spirit acts as the "earnest" (a down payment or guarantee) of the full inheritance, guaranteeing the completion of God's purchase. Furthermore, the sealing is specified to last "unto the day of redemption" (Ephesians 4:30), indicating a permanent mark.

3. Guarded by the Father's Power

The believer is not left to their own failing strength but is actively preserved by God Himself.

  • "Who are kept by the power of God through faith unto salvation ready to be revealed in the last time." (1 Peter 1:5)
  • The conviction of the Apostle Paul was based on God’s fidelity: "For I am persuaded, that neither death, nor life, nor angels, nor principalities, nor powers, nor things present, nor things to come, Nor height, nor depth, nor any other creature, shall be able to separate us from the love of God, which is in Christ Jesus our Lord." (Romans 8:38-39). The list of potential obstacles is comprehensive, concluding that nothing in "all creation" can nullify God’s love and saving work.

Self-examination

Since eternal security rests on Christ's work, how can you know if you are genuinely in Christ? Assurance is often distinguished from feelings by looking for the fruit that the Holy Spirit produces in a believer's life.

Distinguishing Assurance from Feelings

Assurance is a settled conviction based on God's promises; feelings are transient and unreliable.

  • Assurance is by Word, not Emotion: True assurance is rooted in the "record" God has given (1 John 5:11). If you have believed on the name of the Son of God, the Scripture is written so that "ye may know that ye have eternal life" (1 John 5:13). The basis for knowing is the promise of God, not how spiritual you feel.
  • The Unreliability of Feelings: Salvation is by faith (Ephesians 2:8), which is the opposite of sight or feeling. Focusing on good days or bad days leads to doubt, as all have sinned and fall short (Romans 3:23).

The Fruit of Repentance and Faith

The presence of the Spirit guarantees salvation, and the presence of fruit provides evidence of the Spirit's dwelling. This evidence is a change in the direction of your life.

  • The Mark of New Birth: The one who is truly born of God will not live in habitual, unrepentant sin, because "his seed remaineth in him" (1 John 3:9). While a believer may fall into sin, the divine nature within causes the person to repent and return to walking in the light (1 John 1:9).
  • Walking in the Spirit: The presence of "the fruit of the Spirit" is evidence of the new life: "But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, longsuffering, gentleness, goodness, faith, Meekness, temperance: against such there is no law." (Galatians 5:22-23).
  • Obedience as Evidence: The love of God and the keeping of His commandments become the natural desire of the born-again person. "And hereby we do know that we know him, if we keep his commandments." (1 John 2:3). This obedience is not a condition for receiving eternal life, but the proof that eternal life has been received.

Common Pitfalls for Assurance

Doubt concerning eternal security often stems from a misunderstanding of the foundation of salvation, leading believers to look in the wrong places for assurance.

1. Confusing Sanctification with Justification

This is the most frequent source of doubt.

  • Justification is the instantaneous, one-time act of God declaring a believer righteous based on Christ's finished work (Romans 5:1). It is the state of being saved.
  • Sanctification is the lifelong, ongoing process of being made more like Christ (Philippians 1:6). It is the evidence of being saved.
  • The Pitfall: Seeking assurance in the daily process of sanctification (your fluctuating holiness and obedience) rather than the fixed status of justification (Christ's perfect righteousness imputed to you) will always lead to doubt. Your security is in the finished work of Christ, not your unfinished work.

2. Misinterpreting Chastening for Condemnation

When a true believer sins, God responds with parental discipline, not eternal rejection.

  • Chastening: "For whom the Lord loveth he chasteneth, and scourgeth every son whom he receiveth." (Hebrews 12:6). God disciplines His children as a sign of their sonship, to call them back to righteousness (Hebrews 12:11).
  • Condemnation: The Scripture clearly states that for those in Christ, "There is therefore now no condemnation to them which are in Christ Jesus, who walk not after the flesh, but after the Spirit." (Romans 8:1).
  • The Pitfall: Believing that a period of divine discipline or a significant personal failure means you have been eternally disowned. The presence of chastening is, paradoxically, a sign that you are a son (Hebrews 12:8).

3. Relying on Emotional Highs or Spiritual Experiences

Assurance is a matter of fact based on God's Word, not a feeling based on subjective experience.

The "record" is that God has given life in His Son (1 John 5:11). The Pitfall: Equating a lack of emotional fervour, powerful prayer experiences, or deep spiritual "feelings" with a loss of God's favour or salvation. Feelings are fickle; God's word is faithful: "If we believe not, yet he abideth faithful: he cannot deny himself." (2 Timothy 2:13).

To maintain assurance, one must continually direct attention away from self-performance and back to the authority and power of Christ's promise.

If You Have Fallen Away

It is possible for a true believer to stumble grievously or to backslide—to temporarily turn from following Christ and walk in disobedience. The Scriptures address this experience directly, emphasizing God's faithfulness even when the believer is unfaithful.

1. The Reality of the Backslider

The Bible acknowledges that believers can stray from the path. This spiritual distance is a severe threat to their temporal walk and rewards but does not undo their eternal standing.

  • The Path of Sin Brings Death (Temporal): "Then when lust hath conceived, it bringeth forth sin: and sin, when it is finished, bringeth forth death." (James 1:15). This death refers to the loss of fellowship, usefulness, and, in severe cases, the end of the believer's physical life (as seen in 1 Corinthians 11:30 concerning the misuse of the Lord's supper). However, this physical or relational "death" does not equate to eternal damnation.
  • The Need for Repentance: The backslider is commanded to repent and return. "If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us our sins, and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness." (1 John 1:9). This confession is not for a new salvation, but for the restoration of fellowship that was broken by sin.

2. The Faithfulness of God in Your Absence

The eternal security of the backslidden believer is anchored in the reality of God’s character, which remains constant even when the believer's commitment wavers.

  • God Abides Faithful: "If we believe not, yet he abideth faithful: he cannot deny himself." (2 Timothy 2:13). God's faithfulness is not contingent upon the believer's continuous faith; it is fixed on His own nature and His covenant promise through Christ. His work (the justification and sealing) is complete.
  • The Discipline of Sonship: When a true believer falls away, they do not merely walk away unnoticed. They experience the chastening of the Father (Hebrews 12:6-8), a painful process intended to restore them, proving that the relationship of Father and son remains intact. If the believer were not truly saved, there would be no reason for God to chastise them.
  • The Intercession of Christ:Your security is also held by the ongoing ministry of Jesus Christ in heaven, who "ever liveth to make intercession" for those who come unto God by him (Hebrews 7:25).

If you are experiencing a state of being "fallen away," the teaching of eternal security is not a license for sin, but a divine assurance that when you return to the Lord through repentance, your position as a child of God is secure. Your return is already made possible by the Spirit whom God sealed you with unto the day of redemption.
